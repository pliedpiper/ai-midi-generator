This semester has been wild from a tools perspective. I had used AI before in classes where it was allowed, but this was the first semester where I really saw how powerful these tools could be. I started with Claude Code over the winter, and it was mind-blowing to see how much faster I could build and debug. Later, I switched to ChatGPT Codex, which fit my workflow even better. If I could suggest one thing to the school, it would be to offer a junior- or senior-level computer science course focused on agentic coding. Students could spend around $20 a month on a subscription to one of these tools and learn how to use it effectively in a professional way. Looking back, I do not think I would have gotten hired this semester without developing these skills and being able to show them on my resume. It feels like these tools have given us a new kind of superpower, and I have only begun to understand what they can really do.
